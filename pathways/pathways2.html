Toastmasters Paths & Projects

DYNAMIC LEADERSHIP

This path helps you build your skills as a strategic leader. The projects on this path focus on understanding leadership and communication styles, the effect of conflict on a group and the skills needed to defuse and direct conflict. These projects also emphasize the development of strategies to facilitate change in an organization or group, interpersonal communication and public speaking. This path culminates in a project focused on applying your leadership skills.

PROJECTS

LEVEL 1

Evaluation and Feedback

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to learn about giving and receiving feedback.

Overview: Deliver your first speech to your club. Your speech may be on any topic and should be 4 to 6 minutes in length.

Introduction to Toastmasters Mentoring

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to clearly define how Toastmasters envisions mentoring.

Overview: Write and present a 5- to 7-minute speech about a time when you were a protégé. Share the impact and importance of having a mentor. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

Understanding Your Leadership Style

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to identify your primary leadership style or styles.

Overview: Complete the Discover Your Leadership Style questionnaire. Consider how your leadership style impacts the people around you and how you can adjust it to more effectively lead people with styles different from your own. Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of your leadership style or leadership styles in general. You may choose to discuss your style preferences when working with others, your style and how you can adapt it to situations, or leadership styles in general and how they impact a group.

LEVEL 2

Active Listening

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to demonstrate your ability to listen to what others say.

Overview: At a club meeting, fulfill the role of Topicsmaster. As Topicsmaster, comment on each speaker’s Table Topics® speech to demonstrate your active listening skills. For example, you might say, “Thank you. That was a compelling opinion on the benefits of gardening. I understand you feel strongly that everyone needs to spend some time doing something they love.”

Planning and Conducting Practice Negotiations

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to introduce the basic concepts of negotiation.

Overview: Conduct two role-play negotiations with fellow club members. Each negotiation should be 15 to 20 minutes, including planning, negotiation, and debriefing. Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of your experience with negotiation. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

Understanding Conflict Resolution

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to introduce conflict resolution and your approach to it.

Overview: Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of what you learned about conflict resolution. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

LEVEL 3

Communicating Change

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to practice the skills needed to effectively communicate change to a group or organization.

Overview: Develop a plan for communicating a change to an audience affected by the change. At a club meeting, communicate the change in a 5- to 7-minute speech. You may speak about a real or hypothetical change that affects your club or another group in your life. This speech is not a report on the content of this project, but a speech about how you would or will communicate a real or hypothetical change.

Creating Effective Visual Aids

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to practice selecting and using a variety of visual aids during a speech.

Overview: Choose a speech topic that lends itself well to using visual aids. Create at least one but no more than three visual aids to enhance your presentation. Deliver your 5- to 7-minute presentation at a club meeting.

Using Descriptive Language

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to practice writing a speech with an emphasis on adding language to increase interest and impact.

Overview: You may speak on any topic. Develop a 5- to 7-minute speech describing the topic in detail and present it at your club meeting.

LEVEL 4

Leading in Any Situation

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to identify the leadership skills needed to lead in a variety of situations.

Overview: Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of what you learned about leadership skills. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

Leading in Difficult Situations

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to introduce the skills needed to lead a team through adversity.

Overview: Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of what you learned about leading in difficult situations. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

Managing Online Meetings

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to introduce the skills needed to effectively conduct online meetings.

Overview: Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share some aspect of what you learned about conducting online meetings. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.

LEVEL 5

High Performance Leadership

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to apply your leadership and planning knowledge to develop a project plan, organize a guidance committee, and implement your plan with the help of a team.

Overview: Select a project to complete with a team of at least three other people. Develop a plan, form a guidance committee, and complete your project. Present a 15- to 20-minute speech to your guidance committee to share the results of your project.

Reflect on Your Path

Purpose: The purpose of this project is to review the skills you have learned during your path and consider how you will use them in the future.

Overview: Deliver a 5- to 7-minute speech at a club meeting to share your thoughts on your path and how you will use your skills in the future. This speech is not a report on the content of this project.
